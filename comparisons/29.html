January 27, 2015 Dear Members of the Standards Committee, My name is Eileen O’Rourke, I am the Marketing Administrator for the Virginia Institute of Blacksmithing and work with Dale Morse, an artisan blacksmith, whom I have nominated for Master Artisan of Virginia. From the age of nine, Dale began his love for the trade of artistic metal work and never looked back. His early training took place in the forges of the Booker T. Washington National Monument in Franklin County, VA where he and his family exhibited historically accurate demonstrations of everyday life in the rural 1860’s era tobacco plantation. He started his own blacksmithing business from his home when he was only 14 years of age then continued his education at Warren Wilson College in Swannanoa, NC where he established a blacksmithing shop for the college. Dale furthered his education in blacksmithing at the Penland and John C. Campbell craft schools and to this day continues to be a regular instructor at both Warren Wilson and the John C. Campbell School. In addition, he has studied under Manfred Bredhol of the Vulkanschmiede in Aachen, Germany, Gabrielle Curtolo in San Servolo, Venice, Italy and Joseph Stokes of Shropshire, England. Through his years of experience and his teachings, Dale’s approach is to use a minimum of modern welding preferring traditional joinery techniques that will enhance the aesthetic appeal of his work. His methods show the metal was skillfully manipulated while hot, lending a flowing artistry to the finished piece. As proprietor of Clay Hill Forge, an artisan blacksmithing studio near Waynesboro, VA, Dale has formed ironwork creations that grace homes and businesses in the Southeast United States including Virginia, NC, SC and FL. His work is not unfamiliar to those of us who live locally either, as all of the chandeliers and additional iron work at Devils Backbone Brewery in Roseland, VA was created by Dale. Dale continues to bring blacksmithing education and skill to students of all ages and from many states to the Virginia Institute of Blacksmithing. Clay Hill Forge is the founding partner of the Virginia Institute of Blacksmithing (VIB), Dale is the co-proprietor. The Virginia Institute of Blacksmithing was founded to offer information and instruction in traditional blacksmithing ranging from beginning blacksmithing, technique and project driven classes to advanced certification. Booker T. Washington’s philosophy that Dale learned at the age of nine, is the adage and teaching philosophy at VIB today; ‘Teaching of the Hand and the Mind’. VIB is currently the only regional blacksmithing school that offers a certification program that is designed to train interested students in the art of architectural and ornamental metalwork at a professional level. The program was designed by Dale and is recognized by Blue Ridge Community College. Dale is also a Guild Mentor in the Shenandoah Valley Blacksmithing Guild. Dale is a consummate ornamental blacksmith and truly enjoys sharing his skill and art to others so that they can experience the intricacies of manipulating hot metal to its more artistic form. He is also very eager to discover new skills and techniques, so when he encounters someone in the trade who knows how to do something new or better, he is appreciative and keen to learn. Thank you for considering Dale Morse for the honor of Master Artisan of Virginia, as I feel he exemplifies all the necessary talent and skill to be called “Master Artisan”. Sincerely, Eileen O’Rourke
